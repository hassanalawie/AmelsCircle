De-escalating Your Anxiety Improving Your Mood

Deescalating your anxiety

My anxiety sneaks up on me at random times throughout the day. Some days it’ll be on my way to school other days it’ll be at home. In the beginning I found it incredibly hard to refrain from getting overwhelmed and panicking; however, I now use some techniques to calm myself down.

Understand

Understand what a panic attack is and why it happens. A panic attack is a sudden onset of fear and discomfort that lasts a few minutes. Symptoms of a panic attack include: increased heart rate, sweating, shortness of breath, and trembling. Panic attacks are often caused by excessive stress such as job loss or the death of a loved one. Panic attacks can also be caused by medical conditions or traumatic events.

Recognize

Recognize that you are having a panic attack. Remind yourself that the panic attack is not harmful, and by recognizing that you are having one you are taking away the fear involved. Accept that your body will take a few minutes to get your adrenaline back to normal and reassure yourself that what you are feeling is temporary and will pass.

Take Deep Breaths

Hyperventilating is a symptom of anxiety and although it has been said over and over again, it really helps to take deep breaths and let your mind drift off. Focus on taking in deep breaths through your nose and out your mouth. While taking deep breaths, assure yourself that you are okay.

Limit

Try to limit the consumption of coffee, cigarettes and alcohol. Any drink with a lot of caffeine will make you feel jittery and may ultimately trigger your anxiety. Cigarettes and alcohol may calm you down at first but later on will make you feel jittery, contributing to your anxiety.

Visualize

Panic attacks often come from triggers that overwhelm you. Close your eyes and allow yourself to visualize. Picture something you love doing whether it’s painting in your room or riding a bike around the neighbourhood. This positive image will help you relax and calm down after getting anxious.

Walk

If possible, take a walk. Sometimes when we sit in one spot for too long, we start to overthink. This is why a quick 5-minute walk around the neighbourhood can help you calm down and focus on other things like nature or that weird looking house you’ve never noticed before.
Controlling your anxiety takes time and practice, it will not disappear in one try. This is why you need to continue practicing the techniques that you believe work best for you in order to strengthen them and control your anxiety.

3-3-3

Another technique I’ve found to be helpful is the common 3-3-3 challenge. If you find yourself feeling overwhelmed, whether in public or in private, look around and name 3 objects you see, 3 sounds you hear and finally, move 3 of your body parts. This challenge helps you relax and gets your mind to slow down a bit.

What You Eat

One of the most important things about your mood is your diet. What you eat can contribute to how you feel. Stay away from caffeine and sugary foods/drinks as they can affect your mood. This doesn’t mean you can’t have anything that contains sugar or caffeine but limit it to a small amount. Some healthy and nutritious options I enjoy include whole grain rice, fish, vegetables, fruit and eggs.

Organize

Organize your thoughts. Make charts of things that worry you, for example a school and work schedule for the next week or the courses you need to take in the next year. Organizing information in a chart can help put your mind at ease. Organizing also helps you focus on other aspects of your life, giving you more free time and energy to improve your life in other areas.

Sleep

Sleep is very important to your mood. Harvard Sleep Studies have proven that even slight sleep deprivation has an extensive effect on your mood. Not enough sleep can cause irritability and stress, while sleeping for at least 7 hours can enhance your mood. Create a sleep schedule that allows you to have around 7 hours of sleep. When getting ready to sleep stay away from caffeinated and sugary foods/drinks. Also make sure to stay away from bright lights or screens.

Exercise

From experience anyone can say that exercise makes you feel good. The reason why you should start exercising regularly is that exercise actually increases your level of endorphins. Endorphins are natural mood lifters. Exercise can also help improve your mood by getting your sleep patterns back to normal. Regularly exercising can also reduce stress and symptoms of mental health conditions like depression and anxiety.

Writing

Writing is a great way to improve your mood and mental health. Get a journal for writing down your thoughts and feelings. Writing down your thoughts and feelings helps you gain control of your emotions while improving your mental health. If you struggle with stress, depression, or anxiety, writing in a journal is a good way to alleviate some of those feelings by understanding them more clearly.

Take a bath

Many people claim that taking a hot bath helps improve their mood but is it true? Studies show that heating up your body assists in muscle relaxation which in return boosts your mood. Researchers at the University of Freiburg in Germany discovered that by regularly increasing people’s body temperatures they were able to improve their internal clock which resulted in lessening some of their depression symptoms. From experience, a hot bath has always made me calm down and has helped my chest feel lighter.

Reframing The Way You Think

Our perception of the control we have in our lives can affect our ability to cope. When you feel yourself getting overwhelmed by your thoughts try to reframe them. Instead of thinking “I am always going to fail” try reframing to “this wasn’t my best attempt but there is always another chance”. Remind yourself that everyday there is a new opportunity to try again.
